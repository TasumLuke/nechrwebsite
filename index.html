WSF's Teach 4 Wesea Campaign

Teach 4 Wesea is a community-driven educational initiative under the Wesean Student Federation (WSF). This campaign pairs volunteers with displaced children to ensure uninterrupted education during turbulent times. Volunteers are matched with students based on their educational levels:

  • 📘 To teach middle schoolers, volunteers must have completed high school or above.
  • 🎓 To teach high school students, volunteers must have completed college or above.

Teach 4 Wesea operates both on the ground and online via Zoom, ensuring that children in camps or remote areas continue their education. Volunteers can choose their mode of engagement based on availability and resources.

Teach 4 Wesea provides education through both physical classes in camps and online sessions via Zoom, ensuring that children in remote or conflict-affected areas are not left behind.

Manipur Conflict

Timeline: 2023 - Present

The Manipur Conflict has displaced thousands of children, dividing communities deeply. WSF respects these sensitivities and will not pair:

  • 🚸 Meitei children with Zo volunteers
  • 🤝 Zo children with Meitei volunteers

To ensure harmony, the Kangleipak Wesean Students Union and Zo Wesean Students Union operate independently. While this separation is unfortunate, it is necessary for the campaign's success.

Wesean Regions of Myanmar

Timeline: 2021 - Present

This campaign focuses on the Kachin, Chin, and Naga regions of Myanmar, which have been severely impacted by ongoing conflicts owing to the military dictatorship. Displaced ethnic groups include:

  • 🌍 Zo
  • ⛰️ Kachin
  • 🏞️ Meitei
  • 🌿 Nagas
